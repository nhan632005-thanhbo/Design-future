How to Use
Multisensory Translation System
What this is
A real-time translation of coral reef stress signals into equivalent human sensory experiences, across 6 biological channels simultaneously. When a reef bleaches, what would that feel like in a human body?
How to interact
1
Choose a scenario: Healthy, Heat Stress, Bleaching, or Mass Mortality. Or drag the two sliders manually: SST (sea surface temperature) and DHW (accumulated heat weeks).
2
Watch the reef canvas update in real time. Polyp color, zooxanthellae density, GFP glow, nerve flashes, and ROS particles all reflect the current stress state.
3
Read the translation table: each row maps one coral signal (left) to its human equivalent (right), with a sensation description and the receiving organ.
4
Hover over underlined terms in the table. A tooltip explains the scientific meaning in plain language, with source citation.
6 Translation Channels
● Temperature → Skin temp SST deviation mapped to skin surface °C via TRPV1 threshold
◆ Acoustic SPL → Emotional sound Reef soundscape health mapped to cochlea frequency & emotional state
▲ Nerve Net AP → Micro-vibration Polyp action potential frequency mapped to muscle EMG Hz
◐ UV / GFP → Visual perception Zooxanthellae loss & GFP surge mapped to colour saturation shift
◉ Water Chemistry → Scent & taste pH & ROS levels mapped to olfactory experience via analogue scale
▼ Polyp Contraction → Muscle spasm Coral contraction rate & nerve frequency mapped to %MVC & twitch timing
Reading the translation table
Each row contains:
LEFT
Coral signal: the raw biological value (°C, mV, Hz, pH...) computed from SST and DHW in real time.
BAR
Intensity bar: 0% = baseline healthy, 100% = maximum stress. Colour shifts green → orange → red as severity increases.
RIGHT
Human equivalent: the translated value, sensation description in italics, and the receiving organ in small caps.
About the data
SST, DHW, Fv/Fm, ZX%, and TRPV1 thresholds are grounded in published science. The sensory translations (sound, vision, scent, spasm) are artistic analogues, biological in logic, not clinical measurements. Hover any underlined term for the specific source.
